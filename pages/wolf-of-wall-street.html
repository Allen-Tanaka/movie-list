The Wolf of Wall Street (2013)

Directed by Martin Scorsese

About the Film

The Wolf of Wall Street is based on the true story of Jordan Belfort, a stockbroker who built a empire of wealth, excess, and corruption in 1990s New York. Starting from the bottom of a brokerage firm, Belfort rises to extraordinary riches through aggressive sales tactics, fraud, and a lifestyle of extreme indulgence — until the FBI comes knocking.

Leonardo DiCaprio delivers one of his most energetic and compelling performances as Belfort, with Jonah Hill providing brilliant support as his equally reckless business partner, Donnie Azoff.

Why I Love It

This film is a masterclass in storytelling and performance. Scorsese does not glamorise Belfort's life — he puts you inside it and lets you draw your own conclusions. It is outrageously funny in places, deeply uncomfortable in others, and utterly gripping from start to finish.

What I find most fascinating is how it shows the psychology of ambition and persuasion. Belfort had a gift for motivating people and selling a vision — skills that, channelled properly, could have built something great. That tension between talent and ethics is what makes the film so thought-provoking.

Key Details

  • Release year: 2013
  • Genre: Biography / Comedy / Crime
  • Runtime: 180 minutes
  • Starring: Leonardo DiCaprio, Jonah Hill, Margot Robbie
  • Based on: The memoir by Jordan Belfort
  • My rating: 10/10
